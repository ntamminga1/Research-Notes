  • This paper mentions that they have a stopping power equation S(E\( _{i} \)). I believe that they are computing these values as follows. $$ S_{D}(E) = S_{H}(\frac{E}{2}) $$ We can calculate the interactions with carbon and deuterium based on the NIST STAR programs and the technique above. The paper combines the C and D energies as follows $$ S_{D-CD_{2}} (E) = S_{D-C}(E)n_{C} + S_{D-D}(E)n_{D} $$ where \( n_{C} \) and \( n_{D} \) are the target (or catcher) number densities of each particle. I can use this to help calculate the stopping distance of the deuterons in heavy water vapor.
  • Figure 5 of this paper shows a cross section for neutron production v. deuteron energy graph. This peaks with a cross section of \( 10^{-29} m^{2} \) at a deuteron energy of ~2.5-3 MeV.
  • The angular distribution of neutrons depends most on the peak laser intensity and laser pulse duration. The simulation was able to put \( ~10^{4} \) neutrons on a target ~10m away.
