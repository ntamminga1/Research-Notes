I watched the DESY LAP talk.

Here are some notes/questions I had while watching

  • Mainly focuses on data driven optimization of beam parameters
  • What is Bayesian optimization/statistics?
  • They run a Python layer overtop of the DOOCS control system
  • Have a closed feedback loop that adjusts the energy input to the plasma source by changing the polarization
  • They also have a wavefront sensor and motorized beam expander in closed loop to change the beamfront
  • They use a gas target combination of N2 and H2. The N2 creates a downhill gradient into the acceleration region
  • Their optimization tends to focus on N2 concentration, gas density, focus position, and laser energy
  • Read more on Bayesian optimization
  • His system optimizes over 50 iterations at ~1Hz repetition rate, what happens when we have a ~kHz rep rate? How quickly can we analyse and adjust a high rep rate system?
  • Trained an artificial NN on 5000(80:20) set to test robustness of system? I am not entirely sure what they did here
  • Electron beam stabilization related to laser stabilization
  • Next step: Active stabilization at high rep rate (kHz)
  • Building KALDERA laser lab which will be a 3J in 30fs at 1kHz rep rate
