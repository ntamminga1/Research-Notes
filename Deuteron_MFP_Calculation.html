Deuteron Mean Free Path Calculation

Most of the math used in this calculation can be found either from my meeting with Chris or from the Davis and Petrov article. The first step to calculating the MFP will be to find the stopping power. From the Davis and Petrov article, I know that $$ S_{D-X}(E) = S_{H-X}(\frac{E}{2}) $$ Here X will need to be O and D, because we are dealing with a D₂O vapor. Since the PSTAR calculates proton stopping power, which is basically a Hydrogen atom. I will use this in a material of both Hydrogen (to estimate the \( S_{D-D} \) stopping power), and Oxygen. For a proton with incident energy of 2.5MeV (from the Hah and Petrov article) and Hydrogen, PSTAR says that there is a total stopping power of \( 3.238 \times 10^{2} \mbox{\frac{MeV cm^{2}}{g}} \) and a CDSA of \( 4.299 \times 10^{-3} \mbox{\frac{g}{cm^{2}}} \).
