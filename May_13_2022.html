2 meetings happened today.

First, met with Chris and Ricky. Discussed the D-D fusion occuring in the lab. The main question is where are the neutrons coming from? The main goal is to see how far the accelerated deuterons travel in the heavy water vapor before interacting with an additional particle. This may provide the interactions needed to explain the neutrons that are generated. Here are the important things to note for this calculation.

  • Check the mean free path of the deuterons. This is dependent inversely on cross section and number density. Here is the link to the mean free path notes.
  • The pressure of the chamber is ~20 Torr.
  • This paper gives us a graph of the cross section for neutron production for a given deuteron energy.
  • This is a paper on high rep rate neutron production on a very similar laser to that of Wright Patt. Read it.
  • Use NIST pstar and NIST astar for approximate energies of stopping powers for different mediums, such as water vapor. *NOTE This does not have deuterons or heavy water, so use for approximations only*.
  • The stopping power equation can be used to try and adjust the hydrogen to deuterium in our calculations. Try using the dE/dx method in E&M notes from the spring semester.
  • Remember, \( {g/m² \over g/m³} \) leaves us with units of m.
